


  We study multigrid methods for solving linear systems involving Laplacians of graphs.
  Multigrid methods have been and are being intensively studied by the numerical analysis
  community. Still, after more than 25 years of research, there are no satisfactory proofs
  of convergence.  Our approach is an outgrowth of  work of several researchers,
  on combinatorial preconditioners. We prove tight spectral  perturbation results for
  Laplacians, and we apply them to two-level methods, obtaining convergence results under
  natural graph theoretic assumptions. We show that our assumptions are more general than
  assumptions used by other authors. A major open problem is whether a uniform, 'local' two-level
  hypothesis, is enough to prove sufficiently fast convergence for the full $V$-cycle. We give some
  preliminary  results  and plan to address the problem in the thesis.

  The second part of the proposal stems from the observation that three basic NP-complete
  problems, the Hamiltonian Path problem, the Set Packing problem, and SAT, are amenable to an
  algebraization that suggests a restricted but fairly rich framework of algorithmic
  approaches for these problems. Building on this observation, among else: (i) We show the
  hardness of approximating multivariate integrals and the near-optimality (under plausible
  complexity theoretic assumptions) of the currently best known integration algorithm for positive
  functions, (ii) We give a faster algorithm for the parameterized set-packing problem, solving
  efficiently instances for asymptotically larger values of the parameters. (iii) We prove
  results concerning zero-sum subsets of $\Zp^d$. Further results will be included in the thesis.
