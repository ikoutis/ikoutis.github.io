about
I am an Associate Professor of Computer Science at the New Jersey Institute of Technology. I work in spectral graph theory and algorithm design, especially for problems on graphs and hypergraphs, with applications to machine learning, electronic design automation, and computational biology. I am also interested in algebraic techniques for exact algorithms for NP-hard problems.

My work has received best paper awards at ICALP 2017 and ICCAD 2022, and has been supported by the National Science Foundation, including an NSF CAREER award. I earned my PhD in Computer Science from Carnegie Mellon University. Before NJIT, I served on the faculty at the University of Puerto Rico–Rio Piedras and held visiting appointments at ICERM and the Simons Institute.

research highlights
My work on graph Laplacian solvers has pursued two complementary approaches to preconditioning. In joint work with Gary Miller and Richard Peng, I helped develop subgraph preconditioners that led to an asymptotically optimal algorithm for planar graphs and a near-optimal algorithm in the general case. This work significantly advanced the state of the art and was later featured in the Research Highlights section of Communications of the ACM.

A second line of work, based on “super-graph” preconditioners, focused on practical performance. My Combinatorial Multigrid Solver (CMG) remains one of the fastest available solvers for graph Laplacians and has supported applications including hypergraph partitioning in electronic design automation. This work contributed to a Best Paper Award at ACM/IEEE ICCAD 2022.

In a separate line of research, I introduced the method of algebraic fingerprints, which has enabled faster exact and parameterized algorithms for a range of hard computational problems. This framework contributed to major advances on problems such as Hamiltonian Cycle and, in later joint work with Andreas Björklund and Petteri Kaski, led to further record-setting algorithms recognized with a Best Paper Award at ICALP 2017.

teaching
At NJIT, I have been teaching Algorithms, Complexity, Machine Learning and Deep Learning. I teach both at the Newark campus and at the magnificent NJIT facility at Jersey City. In 2020, I received the Ying Wu College of Computing Excellence in Teaching Award.
