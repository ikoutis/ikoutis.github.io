Key Group Comparisons:

  • Market Share: Group 1 (CS-MS & MS-CSP) represents 64.0% of total applications for F25, down from 70.7% in F24
  • Group Composition: MS-CSP is gaining proportional share within Group 1 (from 12.1% to 20.0% of applications), while MS-AI is gaining share within Group 2 (from 17.3% to 33.8%)
  • Selectivity: Group 2 shows a much larger increase in admit rate (+20.5 points vs +9.7 points), indicating a significant reduction in selectivity
  • Yield: Group 1 maintains a higher deposit rate (7.9% vs 4.9%), though both groups show substantial declines
  • Enrollment Trends: Group 1 is projected to account for 72.2% of total enrollment in F25, up from 63.4% in F24, indicating better relative retention despite overall declines
  • Program Resilience: The specialized programs in each group (MS-CSP and MS-AI) are gaining proportional importance within their respective groups

Statistical Significance Analysis:

Deposit Rate Difference Analysis (back-of-the-envelope calculation)

The difference in deposit rates between Group 1 (7.9%) and Group 2 (4.9%) was analyzed using a two-proportion z-test:

  • Group 1: 53 deposits from 668 admissions (7.9%)
  • Group 2: 20 deposits from 409 admissions (4.9%)
  • Z-statistic: 1.88
  • p-value: 0.06 (6%)

Interpretation: There is approximately a 6% probability that we would observe this difference (or larger) due to random chance if the true deposit rates were equal. While slightly above the conventional 5% significance threshold, this suggests the difference is likely meaningful and not just random fluctuation.
